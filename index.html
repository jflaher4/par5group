PARTNERS
TOM CLARK
Tom spent twelve years in South Florida working for Executive Sports. In 2002, Tom joined the PGA TOUR as Executive Director of the Ford Senior Players Championship in Dearborn. He was the Executive Director of the 2007 and 2009 Presidents Cups and the Executive Director for the 2010 and 2011 Schwab Cup Championships at Harding Park. Tom was also the Executive Director of the TOUR Championship at East Lake for six years before moving to PGA TOUR headquarters in Ponte Vedra.
TIM FLAHERTY
As a former basketball coach, Tim believes strongly in preparation, discipline and execution, principles essential to managing multi-faceted spectator events. He spent 20 years at the United States Golf Association where he was Senior Director, U.S. Senior Open and U.S. Women’s Open Championships. After graduating from Amherst College he played basketball in Ireland and helped create the Belfast United program which fostered cross-community relations in Northern Ireland.
BRIAN FLAJOLE
Brian has a diverse background in event management having been in leadership for two U.S. Olympic Trials, the Amgen Tour of California bicycle race, the AVP Pro Beach Volleyball Tour and for a decade, the Longs Drugs Challenge LPGA tournament. He was the Championship Director of the 2015 U.S. Senior Open at Del Paso CC and the 2016 U.S. Women’s Open at Cordevalle. Brian was Tournament Director of the Boeing Classic on the PGA Tour Champions for six years.
DOUGLAS HABGOOD
Douglas has great passion for marketing and staging events to provide new and unique experiences for all audiences. Since graduating from the University of South Carolina, he has been immersed in every aspect of international professional golf management. Armed with a creative mind and fun-loving mentality, Douglas welcomes every opportunity to engage with talented teams to deliver groundbreaking features to high-profile athletic competitions.
SEAN SOVACOOL
With more than 30 years managing professional golf events, Sean has led teams for 27 USGA Championships and more than 150 tournaments across the LPGA, PGA, Champions, and Korn Ferry tours. He began his career with Executive Sports focused on operations, marketing and sales. In 2001, Sean joined Bruno Event Team, where he was head of the Golf Division for more than 20 years, overseeing ten events annually for the USGA and across all Tours.
